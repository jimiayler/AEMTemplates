Scan to electronic health records (EHR)

Paper-based workflows are still essential in today's hospitals. KYOCERA Multifunctional Printers increasingly facilitate the capture and conversion of documents to EHRs.

This is how it works:
  • Save duplication of records and resources by using KYOCERA Software Applications (HYPAS).
  • Simple press-button digitalisation of statutory patient files and obligatory documentation makes it easier for hospitals to adhere to legal process guidelines.
  • If a patient is moved from one ward to another, their current medical status can simply be added to their EHR using a local device.

European Commission: eHealth Action Plan 2020

One of Europe’s key governing bodies, the European Commission, has acknowledged the benefits of digitalisation and introduced a regulation which requires EHR (electronic health records) to be rolled out across Europe by 2020.

Solution for smartphones & tablets: Data mobility

Action packed
KYOCERA's mobile solutions for smart phones and tablets do away with the need for staff to return to base to make reports from handwritten notes or printed documents. Instead on- device report and treatment forms can easily be completed and sent directly and securely to a selected printer.

At the touch of a finger
In contrast to wordy descriptions of injuries or lesions, photos are shot and simply press- buttoned off with any other data directly into the patient's folder – inside the hospital or from outside. KYOCERA's app benefits everyone – even the patient.

Whitepaper KYOCERA mobile solutions

Advantages of mobile solutions:
  • reduces administrative workloads
  • less paper consumption
  • quick capturing via touch
  • easier to monitor patient's condition

Case Study: University Hospital RHTW Aachen

Masterminding data flow
Aachen's ultra modern University Clinic took part in a KYOCERA pilot project starting 2010. This was to digitalise and transfer the medical content of patient-related paper documentation to electronic health records (EHRs). Beside patient admission data, the compilation of individual medical records is a labour-intensive and time-consuming process. With over 160,000 patients a year, it is also a massive data flow challenge.
